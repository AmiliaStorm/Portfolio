Protein Structure Analysis: AGER

A bioinformatics project exploring the AGER protein through sequence information, biological databases, genetic variation, experimentally determined structures and AlphaFold2/ColabFold structure prediction.

The question

What biological information can we combine to understand AGER?

The project brought together sequence information, gene annotation, genetic variants, experimentally determined protein structures and computational structure prediction.

My role

Database research, analysis and scientific interpretation

I explored several biological databases, compared available structural information and evaluated predicted protein models and their confidence.

The output

A structured analysis of sequence, variants and protein structure

The project combined biological interpretation with figures, structure-prediction outputs and documented research findings.

Structure Prediction Results

Predicted structure confidence Model ranking, sequence coverage and predicted confidence.
01

Model confidence varied across the sequence

Confidence plots help identify which regions of a predicted structure are likely to be modelled reliably and which regions should be interpreted cautiously.

02

Multiple models allowed structural comparison

Comparing ranked models provides insight into whether the predicted fold remains consistent across different model outputs.

03

Database evidence provided biological context

Structure predictions were interpreted alongside information from gene, protein, variant and structural databases rather than being treated as isolated results.

!

Important limitation

Predicted protein structures are computational models. Confidence scores and available experimental evidence should always be considered before drawing biological or clinical conclusions.
